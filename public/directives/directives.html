The service chemotion-repository offers technical infrastructure to collect and share research data for chemical sciences. The data storage is accomplished by the service and all data are stored on servers of the KIT. The service allows a qualified realization of the recommendations of the German research foundation for the storage of research data.
Service provider is the Institute of Organic Chemistry (IOC) in collaboration with the Steinbuchs Centre for Computing (SCC) and the Library at the KIT in Karlsruhe.

1. General

1.1 Authorization, registration and log-off

Academic institutions are automatically recognized through the domain part of the email the user entered for registration. The users may use the service after their registration and confirmation of their email address. If the user's academic institution has not been properly recognized, the user shall communicate the error to the chemotion-repository. An email will also be sent to confirm the validity of the email address every time the user updates its email address on the portal and once a year. The administration of the individual authorization to use the service is carried out by the Institute of Organic Chemistry (KIT). The service providers will consider their right to check the correctness of the given data with respect to the affiliation and data of the user's registration. The log in and log off to the service is available via the website: chemotion-repository website.

1.2 Readability and availability of the stored data

Research data are stored at the Steinbuch Centre for Computing (SCC) guaranteed until 2028. The chemotion-repository intends to prolong this period successively by additional contracts with the SCC but the decision on any prolongation and the duration will be made solely by the two partners chemotion-repository and the SCC. The SCC makes every effort to ensure the best possible technical prerequisites for a long life of the data (best effort) with the available resources. These include, inter alia, adequate air conditioning, uninterrupted power supply, and building protection.

1.3 Admission

The user accesses the chemotion-repository after registering on the website and successfully logging on. When registering, the operator checks the data and the agreement to use the service with the respective home institution. The user can access his data at any time as long as he has a valid access.

1.4. Protocols

HTTPS is enforced at all time.

1.5 Deletion of files and content by users

Files (content unpublished) that are stored in the repository in general can be deleted or changed by the user at any time. Files and data which are published (content published) cannot be deleted or changed anymore. The user agrees that those data (which are selected by the user individually) will be registered at DataCite (with retrieval of a DOI) and Pubchem (Pubchem substance SID). Authors should contact the repository curator at chemotion@ioc.kit.edu if they need to retract or amend published content.

1.6 Maximum of data storage

The data size per user is limited up to date to 10 GB. The operator will adapt allowed data size if this will be necessary in future.

1.7 Costs and fees

There are no fees or costs for the users

1.8 Support via chemotion

The provider will offer service for the use of the repository as long as the resources allow the support. The user is expected to familiarize with the given website and its applications. Support is available via the email address: chemotion@ioc.kit.edu.

2. Data content

2.1 Responsibility for data contents

There are two types of contributed contents, unpublished and published contributed contents.
User contributed content (unpublished): the user may upload user data which are not visible for other users, this content is visible only if the user is logged in or if he/she shares the content with other users. User contributed content (published): If the user selects single items or item groups to publish, the content will become available as open access for other users. The contents will be visible for the public and registered users will be able to download the content including the provided files. All uploaded contents are stored by the user in person. The data stored by him/her are always linked to the user's account data in the database of the storage system. Even if the account of a user is deleted, the connection of the stored data to the user's username (as defined with the registration) will be conserved.
